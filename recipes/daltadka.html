Dal Tadka

It's not lentil soup or lentil dal it's just dal - period. I was having a hard time choosing which of my momâ€™s many dal recipes to share (she legit made a different dal every night), and I landed with this easy Dal Tadka (also known as chhonk or tempering spices) with a blend of moong and masoor lentils, mainly because it cooks so quickly and it's super adjustable.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup yellow moong dal
  • 1/2 cup red masoor dal
  • 4 cups water
  • 3 green chilies
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 1/2 tsp tumeric powder
  • 1.5 tsp coriander powder
  • salt

Instructions

  1. Add the first eight ingredients to your pressure cooker.
  2. Set pressure cook settings to sealing, High, Natural Release, 7 minutes
  3. Wait for pressure to release, after 10 min if the pressure valve hasn't dropped, manually release the remaining pressure. Check to see if the consistency is how you like it!
  4. Start on your tadka/chhonk (temper your spices) using a tiny tadka pan or small frying pan. Add ghee or avacado oil and get it very hot.
  5. Add the rest of the ingredients gradually. Mixing or swirling when when needed to combine.
  6. Top with lime or cilantro, and enjoy with basmati rice!
